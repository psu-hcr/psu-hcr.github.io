Research

My research focuses on making robotic assistive devices more accessible to everyone. I am currently helping to design and construct a low-cost haptic feedback device that utilizes symbolic signaling to assist users complete a wide range of simpler tasks. This can range from assisting people with disabilities achieve their therapeutic exercises, as well as aiding in remote training. My goal is to be able to help bridge the gap between human-robotic interactions, through using an accessible, effective device.
